Syllogisms are logical argument of statements using deductive reasoning to arrive at a conclusion. 
The questions which are asked in this section contain two or more statements, and two or more conclusions follow these statements. 
One has to find out which of these conclusions logically follows the given statements. 
The statements must be taken true even if they seem to be at variance from the commonly known facts.
